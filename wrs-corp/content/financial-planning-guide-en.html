Business travel and event expenses can have a significant impact on a company's financial health. At WRS Turkey, we evaluate and plan expenditures specifically for the ARAC, EMEA, LATAM, and NORAM regions. Effective planning and management of these expenses are critical to keeping costs under control, preventing budget overruns, and maximizing return on investment (ROI). Here's a comprehensive financial planning guide for global business travel and event spending:

  • 1. Establish a Comprehensive Policy

      The first step is to create a clear and detailed corporate policy for business travel and event expenses. This policy should inform employees about covered expenses, spending limits, approval processes, and reporting requirements. Your policy should include:
    • Travel Classes and Accommodation Standards: Flight classes (economy, business), hotel categories (3, 4, 5 stars), and daily accommodation limits.
    • Meal and Hospitality Limits: Daily meal allowance per person.
    • Transportation Options: Guidelines for using taxis, car rentals, and public transport.
    • Entertainment and Client Hosting: Specific rules and approval processes for client hospitality and entertainment expenses.
    • Technology and Communication: Coverage of tech equipment and communication expenses during travel.
    • Cash Advances and Corporate Cards: Limits for cash advances and rules regarding corporate credit card usage.
    • Approval Processes: Who is authorized to approve travel and event expenses and up to what limits.
    • Expense Reporting Processes: Requirements for collecting receipts, report formats, and timelines.
    • Definition of Non-Reimbursable Expenses: A clear outline of expenses that will not be reimbursed.

  • 2. Create a Detailed Budget

      Detailed budgeting is the foundation for keeping travel and event expenses under control. Analyze historical data to forecast future spending and allocate specific budgets for each category.
    • Historical Data Analysis: Review previous years' travel and event expenses to identify trends and average costs.
    • Category Breakdown: Categorize expenses such as airfare, accommodation, meals, transportation, venue rentals, AV equipment, marketing materials, etc.
    • Estimated Costs: Estimate costs based on the number of participants, duration, and destination for each trip or event.
    • Contingency Reserves: Include a buffer for unexpected situations (e.g., cancellations, delays) in your budget.
    • Consider Regional Variations: Keep in mind that costs vary across different countries and cities. For example, accommodation in Tokyo may differ significantly from Istanbul.

  • 3. Leverage Technology and Tools

      Utilizing technology in the management of business travel and event expenses boosts efficiency and reduces errors.
    • Travel Management Systems (TMS): Manage flight, hotel, and car rental bookings from a single platform. These systems typically offer corporate discounts and policy compliance features.
    • Expense Management Software: Tools like SAP Concur, Expensify, and Zoho Expense help digitize receipts, automate expense reports, and accelerate approval processes. Most are mobile-friendly, allowing employees to submit expenses on the go.
    • Data Analysis Tools: Use business intelligence (BI) tools to analyze expense data and identify cost-saving opportunities.

  • 4. Manage Supplier Relationships and Leverage Discounts

      Building strong relationships with suppliers and negotiating bulk purchasing agreements can significantly reduce costs.
    • Preferred Suppliers: Establish corporate agreements with airlines, hotel chains, and car rental companies to access discounts and special rates.
    • Bulk Purchasing Power: If planning multiple trips or events, leverage your volume to negotiate better deals with suppliers.
    • Event Venues: Request discounts by entering long-term agreements or booking multiple events with the same venue.
    • Early Booking: Make flight and accommodation reservations as early as possible to benefit from lower prices.

    • Local Laws and Regulations: Some countries may have legal restrictions or ethical rules regarding gifts or client entertainment. Ensure compliance with such regulations.
    • Currency Fluctuations: Consider the impact of exchange rate fluctuations on your budget and apply hedging strategies if necessary.

  • 6. Continuous Monitoring and Reporting

      One of the most important steps in financial planning is the continuous monitoring and reporting of expenses.
    • Real-Time Tracking: Use expense management software to track spending in real time.
    • Periodic Reports: Prepare monthly or quarterly expense reports and compare them with the allocated budget.
    • Variance Analysis: Analyze any deviations from the budget and take necessary corrective actions.
    • Key Performance Indicators (KPIs): Set measurable metrics such as ROI per trip or average travel cost.
    • Employee Feedback: Collect feedback on travel and event experiences to continuously improve processes.

  • 7. Environmental and Social Responsibility

      Today, companies are also expected to incorporate environmental and social responsibility into their financial planning.
    • Green Travel Options: Encourage lower carbon footprint options such as train travel when possible.
    • Local Suppliers: Choose local vendors for events to support the local economy.
    • Sustainable Accommodation: Prefer environmentally certified eco-friendly hotels.
